Census Data

Included in the Regina distribution are topology data files for several different censuses of triangulations.

Typically you will not need to download any of these files separately. You can find them by:

  • running Regina and looking in the File->Open Example menu, or
  • looking in /usr/share/regina/examples/ (this location might differ for some installations or GNU/Linux distributions), or
  • downloading Regina's source code and looking in the examples/ subdirectory.

On this page you can find some additional census data files that are too large to ship with Regina. You can also find the usual data files that are normally shipped, which may help users with stripped-down installations or older versions of Regina that did not include them.

Each data file includes a text packet containing a full description of the census as well as its origin and literary references where appropriate. Data from external sources (such as the hyperbolic census data shipped with SnapPea) have been converted into Regina data files.

Notes:

  • Closed orientable census: Other authors (Matveev, Ovchinnikov, Martelli and Petronio) were the first to construct censuses of closed orientable manifolds. Regina's census extends these results by enumerating all minimal triangulations of these manifolds.
  • Closed non-orientable census: Amendola and Martelli independently formed a census of closed non-orientable manifolds of up to 7 tetrahedra, around the same time as Regina's 7-tetrahedron census was released in 2003. Regina's non-orientable census has since been extended to 11 tetrahedra.

Where Regina's censuses overlap those of the authors listed above, the results agree completely.

Related Articles and PhD Thesis

The following papers describe some of the algorithms that Regina implements.
  • Normal surface enumeration:
    • B.B., Quadrilateral-octagon coordinates for almost normal surfaces, Preprint, arXiv:0904.3041, April 2009
    • B.B., Converting between quadrilateral and standard solution sets in normal surface theory, Preprint, arXiv:0901.2629, January 2009
    • B.B., Optimising the double description method for normal surface enumeration, To appear in Math. Comp., arXiv:0808.4050, August 2008
    • Jeffrey L. Tollefson, Normal surface Q-theory, Pacific J. Math. 183 (1998), no. 2, 359–374
  • Decompositions of manifolds:
    • William Jaco and J. Hyam Rubinstein, 0-efficient triangulations of 3-manifolds, J. Differential Geom. 65 (2003), no. 1, 61–168
  • Cellular information and embeddings in S4:
    • Ryan Budney, Embeddings of 3-manifolds in S4 from the point of view of the 11-tetrahedron census, Preprint, arXiv:0810.2346, October 2008
  • Census enumeration:
    • B.B., Enumeration of non-orientable 3-manifolds using face-pairing graphs and union-find, Discrete Comput. Geom. 38 (2007), no. 3, 527–571
    • B.B., Face pairing graphs and 3-manifold enumeration, J. Knot Theory Ramifications 13 (2004), no. 8, 1057–1101

This list is by no means complete. For more relevant papers, see the bibliography in the handbook, or Regina's summary article in Experimental Mathematics.

The author's PhD thesis contains detailed descriptions of some of the topological structures, concepts and algorithms used in Regina. It can be downloaded in either of the following formats.

  • PDF (1.8 Mb)
  • Gzipped Postscript (1.1 Mb)
